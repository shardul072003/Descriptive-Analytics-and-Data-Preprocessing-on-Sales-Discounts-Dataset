# Exploratory Data Analysis: Sales & Discounts 📊

##  Overview
This repository contains a Jupyter Notebook (`Assignment(3).ipynb`) focused on performing descriptive analytics and data visualization on a Sales & Discounts dataset. The project demonstrates the foundational steps of Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) required to understand data distributions, identify outliers, and prepare a dataset for further machine learning or advanced analytics.

##  Key Objectives & Features

The notebook is structured to walk through a complete descriptive analysis workflow:

### 1. Descriptive Analytics (Numerical Data)
* Identification and isolation of numerical columns within the dataset.
* Computation of core statistical measures using `pandas`:
  * **Mean & Median:** To understand central tendencies.
  * **Mode:** To identify the most frequent values.
  * **Standard Deviation:** To measure data spread and volatility.
* Interpretation of these metrics to provide baseline business context.

### 2. Data Visualization
* **Histograms:** Plotting numerical distributions to analyze skewness and visual signs of outliers.
* **Box Plots:** Utilizing box-and-whisker plots to strictly define the Interquartile Range (IQR) and pinpoint extreme values/anomalies in the sales data.
* **Bar Charts:** Visualizing categorical columns to analyze the frequency, count, and distribution of different categories (e.g., product types or discount tiers).

### 3. Conclusion & Insights
* A summarized synthesis of the key findings extracted from both the statistical calculations and the visualizations.

## 🛠️ Technologies Used
* **Python 3**
* **Jupyter Notebook**
* **Libraries:** * `pandas` (Data manipulation and statistical calculation)
  * `matplotlib` & `seaborn` (Data visualization)
